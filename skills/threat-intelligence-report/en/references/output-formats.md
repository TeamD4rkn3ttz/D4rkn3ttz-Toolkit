# Output Formats

## Writing style

- Lead with the assessment, but never make it stronger than the evidence.
- Prefer short, direct sentences.
- Distinguish `confirmed`, `observed`, `claimed`, `assessed`, and `not independently verified`.
- Criticize weak evidence through verifiable facts, not insults.
- Use absolute dates and explicit timezones.
- Place source links near the claims they support.

## Rapid assessment

1. One-sentence verdict and confidence
2. Three to five verified findings
3. Unknowns and caveats
4. Impact and recommended action
5. Sources

## Full report

### Executive Summary

- Verdict and confidence
- Audience or organizational impact
- Immediate action

### Incident Overview

- First observation, target, source, and original claim

### Timeline

| Date, time, and timezone | Event | Source | Status |
|---|---|---|---|

### Technical or Data Analysis

- Sample, IOC, and TTP findings
- Corroboration
- Counterevidence and alternative hypotheses

### Assessment

- Authenticity verdict
- Confidence and rationale
- Risk and harm scenarios

### Recommendations

- Immediate
- Within 24 hours
- Longer term

### Limitations and Intelligence Gaps

### Sources

## Internal briefing

- **What:** one-sentence event summary
- **Why it matters:** relevance to the organization
- **Current assessment:** verdict and confidence
- **Check:** relevant logs, accounts, assets, and customer impact
- **Act:** owner and deadline

```text
#CyberThreatIntelligence #ThreatIntel #OSINT
```

Rules:

- Use `alleged` and `confirmed` precisely.
- If the item is old or reposted, disclose that prominently.
- A short reply should link to the full analysis instead of repeating it.
- Let verified facts carry the criticism; remove personal attacks.
- Obtain user approval before actual publication.

## Carousel or card-news copy

1. Event in one sentence
2. Who claimed what
3. Available evidence
4. Verification result
5. Risk and impact
6. Response or monitoring priorities
7. Sources and limitations

Use one message per card. Label screenshots with source and observation time, and redact personal data.
